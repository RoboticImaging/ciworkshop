01About

Computational imaging deserves a room of its own.

Computational imaging is the co-design of optics, sensors, mathematics and computing — extracting information from light beyond what traditional cameras afford. The field underpins advances in medical imaging, astronomy, space, and robotics.

Australian optics and photonics research is thriving. But optical engineering and computational imaging — design, fabrication, systems integration, and computational methods — do not receive the same institutional recognition or structured teaching as in the US or Europe. The result is a structural gap: people working on imaging systems across universities, CSIRO, defence, and industry share few formal community touchpoints.

Early-career researchers have limited regional venues to present, collaborate, and form a professional identity in the field. Industry lacks an interface with academics working at the edge of imaging research. This workshop is a deliberate attempt to bring that community into one room.

A direct precedent is Bracewell@70 (Macquarie, 2025), which marked seventy years since Ron Bracewell's foundational contributions to Fourier methods in imaging — work done in Australia that shaped aperture synthesis, computed tomography, and image reconstruction. It drew attendees from astronomy, medical imaging, robotics, signal processing and optics, demonstrating clear appetite for focused cross-disciplinary events.

02Programme

A full day, built for depth and exchange.

Invited talks

Plenary talks from Australian leaders in the field

A curated set of invited talks covering the breadth of computational imaging — from optical hardware to reconstruction algorithms to translational impact.

Breakouts

Topic-focused technical workshops

Smaller breakout sessions on diffractive optics, neuromorphic sensing, and imaging system modelling — each led by a domain expert, enabling depth beyond a standard talk.

Posters

Student & early-career poster competition

A dedicated session for student and ECR work, with prizes for best contributions. Open call for abstracts will be announced in late 2026.

Council

Inaugural Australian Computational Imaging Community Council

A structured meeting to set community priorities, long-term direction, and pathways to translational impact. The council established here will take responsibility for governance of subsequent editions.

Panel

Closing panel: emerging directions

An open discussion on where the field — and the Australian community — should head next.

Social

Informal evening at a nearby venue

Continued conversation, less formal. Details closer to the event.
